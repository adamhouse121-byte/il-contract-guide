1) Parties

Names of buyer(s)/seller(s). Only listed parties are bound. Make sure names and signing capacity (trust/LLC/POA) are correct.

2) The Real Estate

Address, legal/PINs, parking/storage. This uniquely identifies what you’re buying—confirm it matches the listing.

3) Price, Earnest, Credits

Purchase price, earnest amount/timing, credits, balance due. Missing earnest deadlines can default the buyer.

4) Seller Contribution to Buyer’s Brokerage Compensation

States if the seller will pay a %/$ toward the buyer’s brokerage fee—or not. Impacts buyer cash unless negotiated.

5) Closing

Target date and place (title company). All other timelines back into this date.

6) Possession

Keys at funding unless you agree to a rent‑back. Property broom clean and vacant.

7) Fixtures & Personal Property

Attached/built‑in items stay unless excluded; personal property only stays if included. TVs vs. brackets and smart devices are common confusion. Full guide →

8) Financing

Pick: (a) contingent with dates/terms; (b) all cash; or (c) may finance but no contingency—can’t cancel for financing if chosen.

9) Homeowner’s Insurance

If buyer can’t get acceptable HO‑3 within the window (often 10 business days), buyer may cancel.

10) Flood Insurance Option

If in a special flood hazard area or unacceptable flood terms, buyer may cancel within time.

11) Statutory Disclosures

Illinois disclosures (property condition, lead-based paint pre‑1978, radon pamphlet). Disclose honestly.

12) Taxes, Prorations & Escrows

How taxes/assessments/HOA are prorated. If improved but not fully taxed, an escrow (e.g., 3% of price) may be held.

13) Attorney Review

Commonly 5 business days to approve, modify, or disapprove. Day 1 is usually the first business day after acceptance. Full guide →

14) “As‑Is”

If initialed, no condition warranties and typically no repair requests. You may still inspect if paired with the correct inspection option.

15) Inspections

Choose: waive; inspect & request limited to material defects; or information‑only (no requests). Strict deadlines. Full guide →

16) Additional Inspections

Termite/WDI report and well/septic rules (repairs/escrows). Often needed for rural properties.

17) Condo/HOA Documents

Buyer receives governing docs, budgets, assessments; often has a review/cancel window if unacceptable.

18) Deed

Proper deed (often warranty deed) delivered; transfer taxes per local rule.

19) Municipal Requirements & Transfer Taxes

Who handles city inspections, stamps, water bills, and required compliance repairs.

20) Title Commitment & Insurance

Seller provides title commitment with extended coverage and must cure or insure over unacceptable exceptions.

21) Plat of Survey

Real boundary survey (not a mortgage inspection). Important for fences, sheds, driveways.

22) Damage or Condemnation

If damaged or condemned before closing, buyer may terminate or take proceeds and close.

23) Final Condition & Walk‑Through

Property delivered broom clean and in substantially same condition; final walk‑through confirms.

24) Seller Representations

Seller makes representations (permits, easements, hazardous materials, special assessments). If they become untrue, there may be a right to terminate.

25) Business Days & Hours

Typically Mon–Fri, 8:00 a.m.–6:00 p.m. CT. Deadlines landing on weekend/holiday push to next business day.

26) Electronic Signatures

Electronic signatures and delivery are valid.

27) Directions to Escrowee

How earnest money is held/released; interpleader if there’s a dispute. Keep your escrow receipt.

28) Notices

Valid delivery (personal, mail, fax, email, overnight) and when notice is considered delivered. Use the addresses/emails listed in the contract.

29) Time is of the Essence & Fees

Deadlines matter. Prevailing party may recover attorney fees if allowed by the form.

30) Dual Agency Confirmation

Confirms dual agency and consent if applicable.

31) Sale of Buyer’s Real Estate

Home‑sale contingency with kick‑out. Seller can give notice to waive by deadline (often with extra earnest) or cancel.

32) Cancellation of Prior Contracts

Confirms release from prior contracts, or sets a date to cancel them.

33) Home Warranty

If checked, seller provides warranty up to a set amount. Doesn’t replace inspections.

34) Alternative Energy Systems

Discloses if solar/etc. are owned, financed, or leased. Docs provided; possible cancel right if terms unacceptable.

35) Possession After Closing (Rent‑Back)

Sets daily rate, deposit (often 2%), utilities, keys, and move‑out date. Get it in writing.

36) Specified Party Approval

Purchase conditioned on approval by a named person/entity within a window.

37) Approved Addendums

Recognizes standard addendums (lead, condo/HOA, well/septic, radon, kick‑out, etc.).

38) Miscellaneous Provisions

Catch‑all items (e.g., 1031 exchange references, new construction, commercial notes).
