Fixtures & Personal Property — What Stays With the Home?

If it’s attached or built‑in, assume it stays unless excluded in writing. Freestanding items don’t, unless written in.

Typical inclusions (confirm your form)

  • Built‑in appliances (oven/range, built‑in microwave, dishwasher)
  • Wall‑mount TV brackets (not always the TV) and mounted speakers
  • Smart thermostat and integrated smart‑home hubs wired into systems
  • Light fixtures, ceiling fans, curtain rods
  • Garage remotes, mailbox keys, irrigation controllers

Typical exclusions (unless written in)

  • Freestanding refrigerator, washer/dryer
  • Countertop/portable appliances
  • Patio furniture, grills, potted plants
  • Televisions (screens)

Gray areas to call out explicitly

  • Window treatments: Are the curtains staying? Rods usually do.
  • Smart devices: Doorbells/cameras/locks—if removed, is like‑kind replacement required?
  • EV chargers: Hardwired vs. plug‑in; note ownership & removal.
  • Water softeners / propane tanks: Owned vs. rented; disclose leases/assumption steps.
  • Landscaping/outdoor: Anchored structures, irrigation timers, lighting.

Walk‑through checklist

  • Kitchen & laundry appliances you expect to remain
  • Mounted items (TV brackets, speakers, shelves)
  • Window treatments (rods + curtains)
  • Smart devices (thermostat, doorbell, cameras, garage keypad)
  • Outdoor systems (irrigation timers, landscape lighting)

Buyer tips

  • If you want it, write it in.
  • Get model numbers for appliances you expect to stay.
  • Photograph included items at offer time.

Seller tips

  • Exclude in writing anything you plan to take.
  • Disclose rentals (softener/propane) and transfer steps.

Write‑it‑in language examples

  • Keep appliances: Kitchen refrigerator, washer, and gas dryer to remain.
  • TV/mount clarity: Televisions excluded; wall‑mount brackets to remain. Any holes to be patched and painted.
  • Smart devices: Existing smart thermostat and Ring doorbell to remain; seller to transfer control/app credentials at closing.
  • Window treatments: All curtain rods and curtains currently installed to remain.

FAQ

If the seller removes a smart thermostat, do I get a replacement? If not excluded and considered a fixture, expect a like‑kind replacement or a credit—negotiate via attorney review.

What if the seller took the curtains? If not written in, curtains are often personal property—write them in if you want them.
