Inspections in Illinois — Options, Deadlines, and Scripts

Pick the right inspection path, hit your deadlines, and use clear language for repair or credit requests.

Common inspection pathways

  1. Full inspection contingency — request repairs/credits or cancel per terms.
  2. Information‑only — inspect but agree not to request repairs; may still cancel within the window (verify your form).
  3. As‑Is with inspection right — inspect and cancel within the deadline; no repair/credit requests.
  4. No inspection — waive the right entirely (rare and risky).

Add‑on inspections

  • Radon, termite/WDI
  • Sewer scope (older homes)
  • Well/septic where applicable
  • Lead‑based paint awareness for pre‑1978 homes

Timeline & logistics

  • Schedule immediately after acceptance.
  • Submit any requests in writing before the deadline.
  • Plan 1–3 hours on site; expect a same‑day/next‑day report.

Requesting repairs vs. asking for a credit

  • Repairs — good for straightforward fixes you want done before closing. Ask for licensed contractors and invoices.
  • Credits — prefer when scope is uncertain or timing is tight; you choose contractor after closing. Applied at closing.

“Material defects” (plain English)

Many forms limit requests to significant items affecting health/safety, structure, or major systems. Cosmetic issues are usually excluded. Confirm your language.

Re‑inspection

If repairs are agreed, the buyer (or inspector) may re‑inspect before closing to confirm completion—add this right in writing.

Scripts & templates

  • Repair request: Buyer requests licensed electrician to replace Federal Pacific panel with new [brand], including permit and final inspection, by [date]. Contractor invoice to be provided prior to closing.
  • Credit request: In lieu of repairs for roof wear noted in Sections 3.1 and 3.4 of the inspection report, buyer requests a $3,500 closing credit.
  • Information‑only acknowledgement: Buyer conducted inspection for information only per contract and makes no repair requests; buyer proceeding to closing.
  • As‑Is confirmation with right to cancel: Buyer conducted inspection pursuant to As‑Is selection; buyer elects to proceed with no repair/credit requests.

FAQ

Can I ask for repairs on “as‑is”? Usually no—unless both sides agree to modify the contract in writing.

If the seller refuses all requests, can I cancel? Only if your contingency and timeline allow it. Check your contract.

Should I attend the inspection? Yes—ideally for the final 30–60 minutes to walk through findings.
