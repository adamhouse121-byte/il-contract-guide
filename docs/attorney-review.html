Attorney Review in Illinois — Plain English

What the attorney review window is for, how modifications work, when you can cancel, and timeline traps to avoid.

What it means

After acceptance, each side’s attorney gets a short window (commonly 5 business days) to approve the contract, propose changes, or disapprove. The original contract stays in effect unless the parties sign an amendment.

Deadlines & clock rules

  • Day 1 is usually the first business day after acceptance.
  • Extensions must be agreed in writing—an email between attorneys usually works if the contract allows email notice.
  • If parties can’t agree on attorney changes by the deadline, the form often lets either party cancel.

What can be proposed

  • Language clean‑ups, correcting names/legal descriptions.
  • Repair/credit frameworks separate from inspection clause.
  • Clarifying who pays municipal transfer taxes, association fees, stamps.

Buyer tips

  • Send a clean PDF of the signed contract to your attorney immediately.
  • Ask for a one‑line update each day until approval is done.

Seller tips

  • Decide your redlines in advance so your attorney can move quickly.
  • If you won’t agree to certain changes, say so early to avoid wasting time.
