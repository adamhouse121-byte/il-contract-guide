Earnest Money — Refunds, Disputes & Deadlines

How earnest money works, when it’s refundable, and what to do in a dispute.

Basics

Earnest money shows good faith and is usually due within a few business days of acceptance to the named escrowee (title/attorney/broker). Keep your receipt.

Refundable when?

  • You cancel properly under a contingency (on time and with valid notice).
  • Both sides sign a mutual release.

Disputes

If the parties disagree on who gets the earnest money, the escrowee follows the contract’s directions—often an interpleader (court) if unresolved. Ask your attorney before escalating.

Tips

  • Send notices to the addresses/emails in the contract.
  • Don’t miss the deadline—time is of the essence.
